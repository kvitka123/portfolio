Crochet Machine — Master’s Thesis

Overview

This project explores whether crocheting can be automated by breaking human motion into simple linear and rotational movements and distributing them across three subsystems: a crocheter (rack-and-pinion hook), a tensioner (capstan yarn control), and a holder (rotating textile platform). The system is built from accessible, low-cost components and driven in open loop for an initial proof of concept.

8020 frame Arduino MATLAB PLA 3D prints Motion capture

Abstract & Background

The motivation combines humanitarian and environmental concerns in textile production. The work demonstrates that the core motions of crocheting can be decomposed and actuated mechanically, laying groundwork for more ethical and sustainable manufacturing approaches.

Design & Implementation

Crocheter

Rack-&-pinion linear actuation moves a press-fit crochet hook along a guided track to form loops.

Tensioner

A servo-driven capstan wraps and feeds yarn consistently around the hook to maintain stitch tension.

Holder

A rotating plate secures the textile and advances the work for crocheting in the round; a re-tension arm resets stitches.

Motion Capture & Control

Kinematic experiments indicated that the hook’s dominant motion can be approximated as linear, which informed the simplified mechanical design. A MATLAB pattern generator estimates stitch counts and project dimensions for rectangles and ellipsoids, enabling design-to-fabrication planning.

Results

The prototype successfully produced a single chain stitch end-to-end, validating the underlying mechanism and control sequence.
