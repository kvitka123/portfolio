ErgPAL — Senior Capstone Project

Overview

ErgPAL is an attachment for a rowing erg that delivers physical impulse cues to help athletes train solo while maintaining stroke-rate synchronization. Our team built and tested a compact mechanism and control system to replicate on-water impulses on a standard indoor rower.

ESP‑32 Brushless DC motor Spark MAX Ultrasonic sensing PWM control

My Contributions

  • Designed and prototyped the eccentric-mass impulse mechanism to mimic rowing impulses on the erg.
  • Programmed the ESP‑32 to send PWM signals to a BLDC via a Spark MAX, integrating ultrasonic sensors for position timing.
  • Implemented safety & UX features: emergency stop, LED indicators, and a stroke-rate dial for user control.

How it Works

An eccentric rotating mass provides periodic impulses synchronized to the user’s stroke rate. The ESP‑32 reads sensor data, coordinates motor speed, and drives the BLDC via PWM to a Spark MAX. A simple UI exposes start/stop, rate control, and status LEDs.

Results

Bench tests validated the mechanism’s ability to deliver repeatable impulses and maintain target stroke rates; on‑erg trials confirmed usability and safety with the integrated E‑stop and indicators.
