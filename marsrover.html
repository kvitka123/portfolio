NEU SEDS — Mars Rover

Overview

As part of Northeastern University’s SEDS Mars Rover Team, I worked on mobility concepts and wheel designs for off‑world traversal. We built and tested wheel prototypes and contributed to the rover subsystem integration.

Mobility Wheel design PLA Prototyping FEA CAD modeling Terrain testing




Wheel Design & Engineering

Our goal was to develop wheels capable of traversing rocky and sandy terrain while minimizing slippage and maintaining traction. I focused on lightweight lattice designs inspired by NASA’s Mars Curiosity Rover wheels, combining rigid aluminum hubs with flexible, compliant tread structures for impact absorption.

Using finite element analysis (FEA) and iterative CAD modeling, we optimized the spoke pattern geometry to balance deformation and durability. Each prototype was 3D-printed in PLA for early testing before transitioning to CNC-machined aluminum for the final rover.

We tested our designs on simulated terrain to evaluate traction, rolling resistance, and deformation under load — a critical step before field trials in Utah for the 2022 University Rover Challenge (UROC).

Competition

Utah, 2022

Our team competed in Utah at the UROC Challenge (2022), fielding our rover and testing its systems in desert terrain conditions.
